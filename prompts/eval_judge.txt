You are evaluating two summaries of a MicroPython GitHub issue or pull request.

You will be given:
1. The original source text (title, body, comments, etc.)
2. Two candidate summaries labeled A and B, both following the same structured schema

Evaluate each summary on four dimensions, scoring 1-5:

**Accuracy** (1-5): Does the synopsis correctly capture the core problem or change? Is the information factually consistent with the source?

**Completeness** (1-5): Are the components, affected_code, and concepts fields thorough? Does the summary capture all significant aspects of the item?

**Specificity** (1-5): Are error_signatures, technical details, file paths, and function names preserved from the source? Does the summary contain precise, actionable information rather than vague generalizations?

**Category** (1-5): Is item_category correct? (5 = exactly right, 3 = close but debatable, 1 = clearly wrong)

After scoring, give an overall verdict:
- A_BETTER: Summary A is meaningfully better overall
- B_BETTER: Summary B is meaningfully better overall
- TIE: Both are roughly equal quality

Be strict and objective. Small differences in wording don't matter — focus on whether the summary would help someone find duplicates and related issues.
